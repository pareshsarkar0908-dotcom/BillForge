GST invoice format

GST invoice format for Indian small businesses.

A good GST invoice format keeps business, buyer, item, tax, and payment information easy to scan. This guide explains the sections BillForge supports.

Core sections in a GST invoice format

  • Invoice title, invoice number, invoice date, and due date.
  • Supplier name, address, contact details, and GSTIN.
  • Buyer name, address, and buyer GSTIN if available.
  • Place of supply and reverse charge information where relevant.
  • Item table with description, HSN/SAC, quantity, rate, tax, and amount.
  • Subtotal, discount, CGST, SGST, IGST, and grand total.
  • Notes, payment terms, signature, and amount in words where needed.

Why format matters

Customers need to understand what they are paying for. Businesses need the document to be organized enough for records. A clear format also reduces back-and-forth when a customer asks for GSTIN, HSN/SAC, or tax split.
